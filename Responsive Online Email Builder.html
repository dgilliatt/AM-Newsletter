We Work Like a Blog

Social Media & Senior Living? You Bet.

Senior living is a relationship business. It just is. Relationships between staff and residents. Relationships between your community and the larger community. And, yes, relationships between your sales team and age- and income-qualified prospects. Can social media play a part in developing and nurturing those relationships? It can. IF you have a thoughtful approach to what you post, where you post it and what you expect to get out of the effort.

“A well-planned social strategy isn’t just about posting on Facebook once in a while,” says Angell Marketing’s digital content manager, Nathalie Warner. “It’s about thoughtfully representing your brand in a way that encourages engagement. Authentic engagement from residents, family members and staff is what opens the door to prospects.”

What’s on the Front-Burner?

Fostering a Referral-Rich Mindset

In the world of sales, the term “low-hanging fruit” can induce eyerolls. (Let’s face it, it’s a little trite). But in the world of senior living marketing and sales, it’s difficult to find a better/cheaper/faster/easier lead than one that results from a resident referral. So … maybe it’s not so tired after all? Grab a basket and start picking. “Putting the residents into a referral mindset means engaging them in creating the community THEY WANT,” says Angell Marketing’s VP director of client strategy. “Who better to advocate for the amenities, activities and overall lifestyle you offer than happy residents?” Angell Marketing has developed a comprehensive resident referral program called CONNECT that puts residents, family members, employees, vendors and friends in a referral mindset – making them the ambassadors to your brand (and a handy extension of your sales team). See how the CONNECT program worked for a senior community in Phoenix.
